INCIDENT REPORT
Asset: Reciprocating Air Compressor C101 (Unit A, Instrument Air)
Incident Date: 2025-02-08, 02:40
Severity: High (unplanned trip, no injuries)
Reported By: Shift Operator, Zone 3

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Compressor C101 tripped on high discharge temperature (alarm at 165 C, trip at
178 C). Loss of instrument air pressure dropped the header from 7.0 bar to 4.2 bar
before the standby compressor C102 auto-started and recovered the header.

IMMEDIATE CAUSE
Discharge temperature excursion caused by a fouled intercooler and a partially
stuck discharge valve on the 2nd stage.

UNDERLYING CAUSE
Intercooler cooling-water flow was restricted by scale build-up. The 2nd-stage
discharge valve plate showed fatigue cracking — a repeat of the failure mode seen
on C101 in 2024-09 (see prior incident INC-2024-0731).

IMPACT
- 18-minute partial loss of instrument air.
- Two field control valves drifted to fail-safe; no process upset.
- No environmental release, no injury.

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS
- Replaced 2nd-stage suction and discharge valve assemblies.
- Cleaned and descaled the intercooler; restored cooling-water flow.
- Lowered the high-temperature pre-alarm to 155 C for earlier warning.

RECURRING FAILURE NOTE
This is the second 2nd-stage valve failure on C101 within 6 months. Recommend a
root-cause review of valve material selection and intercooler water treatment for
Unit A reciprocating compressors.

STATUS: C101 returned to service 2025-02-09 after valve replacement.

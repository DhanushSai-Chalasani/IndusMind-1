MAINTENANCE REPORT
Asset: Centrifugal Pump P101 (Unit A, Cooling Water Circuit)
Report Date: 2025-03-14
Technician: R. Mehta (Mechanical Maintenance)
Work Order: WO-2025-0412

SUMMARY
Pump P101 was taken offline following a high-vibration alarm on the drive-end
bearing. Vibration trend showed a steady rise from 2.1 mm/s to 7.8 mm/s RMS over
six weeks. On disassembly the drive-end bearing (SKF 6312) showed severe outer-race
spalling consistent with bearing wear and inadequate lubrication.

FINDINGS
- Drive-end bearing: outer-race spalling, confirmed bearing wear failure mode.
- Lubrication: grease was degraded and contaminated with cooling-water ingress.
- Mechanical seal: minor weeping, within tolerance, not replaced.
- Coupling alignment: 0.18 mm offset, slightly above the 0.10 mm spec.

ACTIONS TAKEN
- Replaced both drive-end and non-drive-end bearings.
- Re-greased with the specified high-temperature lithium-complex grease.
- Re-aligned the motor-pump coupling to 0.06 mm offset.
- Reset vibration baseline; post-repair vibration 1.4 mm/s RMS.

ROOT CAUSE
Primary: bearing wear accelerated by water-contaminated lubricant.
Contributing: coupling misalignment increasing bearing load.

RECOMMENDATIONS
- Inspect bearing seals on the sister pump P102 (same service, same age).
- Reduce regrease interval from 6 months to 3 months for Unit A cooling pumps.
- Add P101 to the monthly vibration route.

STATUS: Returned to service 2025-03-15, operating normally.
